El - nido... A Haven for beach lovers

El Nido is a municipality on the northernmost tip of the province of Palawan, characterized by awe-inspiring natural beauty, from dazzling lagoons, white sand beaches, rocky islets, towering limestone cliffs, and glasslike waters teeming with abundant marine life. No wonder El Nido has earned nothing but praises from visitors, and numerous accolades of being one the best island and beach destinations in the world. El Nido also encompasses over 40 islands peppered across the Bacuit Archipelago, each with its own unique geological wonders but the same natural majesty. And everywhere you look, the scene is almost otherworldly — its surrounding waters are a stunning mix of blue-green colors, its palm-fringed beach pockets have ultra-powdery sands, and its marine protected areas offer some of the best diving experiences in the entire Philippines. A Carabao And Its Cart

Carabao, known as "kalabáw" in Filipino, "kábaw" in Cebuano, and "nuang" in Ilocano, refers to the water buffalo species (Bubalus bubalis) in the Philippines. In Malaysia, water buffalos are called "kerbau" in Malay and hold special importance as the official animal of the state of Negeri Sembilan. These animals were introduced to Guam from the Philippines during the 17th century and have gained significant cultural significance to the Chamorro people, unofficially becoming the national animal of Guam.

Bangkas on The Beach

Bangka is a term used in the Philippines to refer to various native watercraft. Originally, it denoted small double-outrigger dugout canoes used in rivers and shallow coastal waters. However, since the 18th century, its definition has expanded to include larger lashed-lug ships with or without outriggers. The term "bangka" is used consistently throughout the Philippines, but it encompasses a diverse range of boats specific to different regions. In Spanish, it was spelled as banca, panca, or panga (m. banco, panco, pango). An older, archaic term for these watercraft is sakayan (also spelled sacayan).

Sawali Making

Sawali is the craft of weaving split bamboo into mats typically seen in nipa hut walls. Today the mats are used for various other purposes, such as flooring or for wall décor, symbolizing a stylish cultured appreciation. It is also popular for multi purpose baskets and handbags.

Nipa, also called Sasa or Pawid in other parts of Luzon, is a type of palm found mostly in tidal streams in brackish swamps and muddy banks throughout the Philippines. It is considered one of the most important economic Philippine crops as it has many uses.
